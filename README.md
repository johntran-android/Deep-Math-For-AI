# 🧠 Deep Math for AI & Machine Learning

Welcome to my digital garden! This repository is a raw, unpolished, and continuously updating log of my deep dives into the foundational mathematics of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning.

## 🎯 The Philosophy: Why this repo exists?
In an era where it's easy to just call an API or import a high-level library, I believe true engineering power comes from understanding the bottom-up mechanics. This repo is my commitment to the **First Principles** and the **Feynman Technique**. 

I don't just read textbooks; I deconstruct them, argue with the theorems, spot edge cases, and prove them from scratch. 

## ⚠️ Note on Formatting (The "Raw" Look)
You will notice that these notes are **not** perfectly typeset in LaTeX. This is intentional. 
These are my raw, authentic notes typed out during intense study sessions. I use markdown code blocks to format mathematical derivations (e.g., integrals, expectations, and matrix operations) to maintain the authentic "engineer's scratchpad" feel rather than an AI-generated textbook.

## 🛠️ The Workflow & Tooling
I am a firm believer in "Dogfooding" my own products. This entire repository is generated and maintained through my custom workflow:
1. **Study & Diagramming:** I use mind mapping tools and my custom-built app, **StudyBoard** (an app designed specifically around the Feynman technique), to break down complex theorems.
2. **Automated Pipeline:** I wrote a custom Python automation script that parses my local study trees, extracts the raw text and screenshots, formats them into Markdown, and auto-syncs them to this GitHub repository. 

## 📚 Current Focus Areas
Here are the core subjects and textbooks I am actively dissecting:

### 1. Statistical Inference
* **Core Text:** *Statistical Inference* by George Casella & Roger L. Berger.
* **Topics:** Probability theory, distributions, UMVUE, Cramer-Rao Lower Bound, Rao-Blackwell Theorem, and Hypothesis Testing.

### 2. Numerical & Convex Optimization
* **Core Texts:** *Numerical Optimization* by Jorge Nocedal & Stephen J. Wright, and *Convex Optimization* by Stephen Boyd.
* **Topics:** Unconstrained optimization, Line search methods, Trust-region methods, Hessian approximations, and Duality.

### 3. Linear Algebra (Advanced Foundations)
* **Core Curriculum:** MIT 18.06 (Prof. Gilbert Strang).
* **Topics:** Vector spaces, eigenvalues/eigenvectors, Singular Value Decomposition (SVD), and matrix calculus.
